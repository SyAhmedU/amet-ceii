Why CEII

Every department has a
founder in it — we help
them build.

AMET University spans 18 departments and thousands of students across engineering, management, computer science and the sciences. Great ideas come from all of them — and most never get built. CEII exists to change that: a founder-first environment open to every discipline on campus.

We pair the breadth of a full university with the tempo of a modern venture studio — mentors, capital introductions, lab access and a real path from idea to company. AMET's maritime heritage is a distinctive edge, not a limit.

Programs

Four stages from idea to company

A staged voyage — each program meets founders exactly where they are, and hands them off to the next with momentum.

01 — Pre-incubation

Ideate

Validate the idea. An 8-week sprint on problem-fit, market research and a first prototype — open to any discipline.

Idea → Prototype ↗ 02 — Incubation

Incubate

Build the product. Six months of mentorship, lab and workshop access, and pilots with industry partners.

Prototype → Pilot ↗ 03 — Acceleration

Accelerate

Scale & raise. Investor readiness, go-to-market, and warm introductions to the capital network.

Pilot → Revenue ↗ 04 — Always-on

Connect

Community, events and student ventures through the Institution's Innovation Council (IIC) — the pipeline that feeds it all.

The offer

Everything a founder needs, in one place

✦ Mentorship

A hand-matched mentor from industry, tech, finance or academia — plus regular office hours with the CEII team.

◎ Capital

Grant guidance (DPIIT, state seed funds), a curated angel & VC network, and demo-day exposure.

▚ Labs & workshops

Access to AMET's engineering, science and technology labs — including its distinctive maritime facilities — plus co-working space.

⇄ Pilots

Warm routes to AMET's industry partners for real-world pilots and first customers.

§ Legal & IP

Company formation, IP strategy, compliance and contract templates — the unglamorous essentials, handled.

◈ Community

A community of founders, alumni and experts — events, teardown sessions, and a network that outlasts the program.
